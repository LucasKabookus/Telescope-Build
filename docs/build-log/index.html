Build Log (Chronological)

2023-06-10 — Signup for Gifted Independent Study course.

Applied for Gifted Independent Study course at high school.

2023-08-15 — Preliminary schematic made w/ P-mirror cell design.

Drafted components of telescope as preliminary design, began scouting for parts.

LATE 2023 — Parts ordered.

Sourced and financed parts from Agena Astro: P/S mirrors, eyepieces, focuser.

2023-12-25 — Parts delivered/Beginning of construction.

Delivery of P/S mirrors, eyepieces, focuser; beginning of construction.

2024-01-01 — Purchased tube.

Purchased 11" by 48" Quik-Tube from local Home Depot.

2024-01-12 — Began construction of P-mirror cell.

Purchased and sanded plywood into 11" diameter disk (mirror mount); cut second, smaller 10" diameter disk for adjustment screw mounts.

2024-01-16 — Continued construction of P-mirror cell.

Affixed screws + springs onto disks; created adjustable mirror structure.

2024-02-05 — Polishing of disks.

Applied multiple coats of polyeurothane to disks for weather/humidity-proofing.

2024-02-07 — Felt pads + braces purchased.

Purchased components for felt brace to mount P-mirror onto cell.

2024-02-08 — Felt pad mount made.

Anchored braces to wooden exterior of cell; attached felt and attempted to mount mirror → too small.

2024-03-12 — Re-sanded smaller wooden disk.

Resanded the wooden disk aligned w/ P-mirror to better fit.

2024-03-12 — Re-attached felt braces.

Attached felt pads to outer rim of newly sanded disk; P-mirror fit well.

2024-03-17 — S-mirror cell schematic.

Designed the layout for S-mirror cell schematic: spider mount, wooden dowels, angled wooden support block.

2024-04-02 — S-mirror cell parts sourced (1).

Purchased metal dowels, wooden dowels, and spare plywood block from local home depot.

2024-04-04 — S-mirror cell parts sourced (2).

Purchased aligned screw materials, additional supporting nuts + bolts.

2024-04-09 — S-mirror cell construction.

Used saw to angle wooden block at 45° angle to mount S-mirror; bored holes for metal spider dowels.

2024-04-10 — Wooden block finish.

Polyeurothane coats applied to wooden block to prevent weather/humidity warping.

2024-04-15 — Build collimation screws into place.

Drilled holes for collimation screws; attached screws + springs to wooden block mirror mount.

2024-04-16 — Experimented w/ focal lengths.

Used flashlight to measure focus distance on primary/ secondary mirrors to gauge alignment.

2024-04-18 — Attached dowels + mount.

Drilled holes in correct spot on the tube for metal dowels; anchored S-mirror mount + mirror on metal spider.

2024-04-21 — GIS Presentation.

Presented telescope process/progression to peer, mentors at high school.

2024-05/06 — TOOK BREAK.

Halted construction to focus on culminating/AP exams, other academic responsibilites.

2024-06-21 — Began construction of focuser/eyepiece mount.

Measured light alignment to from mirrors to place focuser at the focused image.

2024-06-23 — Continued construction of focuser/eyepiece mount.

Cut hole in the outer fuselage of tube for focuser; mounted focuser in place with screws until fixed.

2024-06-23 — Finalized construction of focuser/eyepiece mount.

Mounted eyepieces to focuser → both fit well.

2024-07-02 — Spraypainted tube.

Spraypainted inside, outside of tube black to avoid unnecessary reflections inside tube.

2024-07-18 — Collimation laser delivery.

Delivery of collimation laser to collimate/align instruments.

2024-07-20 — Collimating telescope.

Mounted mirrors in respective cells + affixed eyepiece; shot collimation laser down eyepiece to measure alignment → poor.

2024-07-23 — Testing finalized mechanics.

Adjusted mirrors in cells to match collimation; held tube to get preliminary views of moon (too shaky for planetary viewing w/o mount).

2024-08-15 — Rocker mount inspiration.

Watched John Dobson rocker box mount design; began drawing up schematic.

2024-08-25 — Sourced plywood.

Sourced birch from local home depot store for rocker mount.

2024-09-01 — Purchased additional components for mount.

Purchased textured laminate ring for az-bearing surface; pivot bolt sourced.

2024-09-03 — Purchased additional components for mount.

Purchased teflon padding for components of mount + handle for easy transport.

2024-09/12 — TOOK BREAK.

Took my longest break during the entire project to focus on growing academic responsibilities.

2025-01/03 — Began rocker box construction.

Built outer wooden frame (ground plate + box itself).

2025-01/17 — Azimuth module construction.

Attached laminate disk to box; placed teflon padding on ground plate for smooth az.

2025-01/18 — Center bolt placement.

Cut/fixed center bolt in place in rocker box for alignment, stability of rotation.

2025-02/03 — Applied finish to rocker box plywood.

Applied polyeurothane + stain to birch to prevent weather/humidity warping.

2025-02/18 — Began building altitude bearing mounts.

Cut out wooden semicircles from birch to rest on outer box, support tube + alt movement.

2025-02/22 — Built altitude disks for telescope tube.

Cut complementary altitude disks for telescope tube.

2025-02/22 — Applied finish to cuts.

Applied polyeurothane + stain to birch semicircles/disks to match rocker box + prevent weather/humidity warping.

2025-02/23 — Attached teflon to cuts.

Attached teflon padding to finished semicircles/disks to provide low-friction movement.

2025-02/23 — COG measured on tube.

Marked center of gravity of finalized telescope tube to gauge where disks should go for smooth alt movement.

2025-03/13 — Finalzied rocker box structure.

Attached ground boards, side panels, semicircle together w/ stiffeners for rigidity.

2025-03/18 — Mount alt disks.

Attached altitude bearing disks to telescope tube to allow rocking movement along rocker box semicircular bearings.

2025-03/19 — Completed rocker box design.

Attached handle for easy transport; finalzied construction.

2025-03/19 — Tested rocker box mobility.

Tested alt/az movement with tube attached → flawless/smooth movement.

2025-04/20 — Presented telescope progression.

Presented (near) finalzied telescope to peers, mentors at high school; good test of disassembly/transport.

2025-05/06 — TOOK BREAK.

Necessary break to accomodate for culminating/AP exams alongside other pressing academic responsibilities at closing of school year.

2025-07/11 — First test!.

Took finished telescope to local park on clear night; captured first sighting of Saturn!

2025-07/13 — Second test!.

Returned to local park; first viewing of Mars!

2025-07/13 — **Telescope completed.**

Official completion of telescope (design + construction + testing) spanning (roughly) 2 years + 33 days (763 days)!
